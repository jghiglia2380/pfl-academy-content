Curriculum Framework

Comprehensive Financial Literacy Standards

A structured approach to equipping students with essential financial knowledge, preparing them for informed decision-making.

State-Aligned Curriculum

Comprehensive standards meeting graduation requirements

Standard 1: Income & Careers

Students explore career options, understand income sources, and learn about the relationship between education, skills, and earning potential.

  • Income sources and career development
  • Education and skill development
  • Tax implications of income

Standard 2: Money Management

Students learn effective money management strategies, including budgeting, saving, and making informed spending decisions.

  • Budgeting and financial planning
  • Banking and financial services
  • Short- and long-term goal setting

Standard 3: Credit & Debt

Students understand the role of credit in personal finance, learn to manage debt responsibly, and build positive credit histories.

  • Credit scores and reports
  • Loan types and terms
  • Managing debt responsibly

Standard 4: Saving & Investing

Students explore saving strategies and investment options to help them build wealth and achieve long-term financial goals.

  • Investment vehicles and strategies
  • Risk tolerance and diversification
  • Retirement planning

Standard 5: Risk Management & Insurance

Students learn to identify potential financial risks and strategies to protect assets through insurance and other risk management tools.

  • Insurance types and coverage
  • Identity theft prevention
  • Financial security planning

Standard 6: Financial Decision-Making

Students develop critical thinking skills to make informed financial decisions and understand the consequences of various financial choices.

  • Ethical financial practices
  • Addressing financial dilemmas
  • Long-term impact assessment

Standard 7: Borrowing Money

Covers insurance basics, premiums, deductibles, and risk management to protect assets and minimize financial losses.

  • Types of loans and interest rates
  • Understanding collateral and creditworthiness
  • Strategies for responsible borrowing

Standard 8: Credit Cards & Online Shopping

Introduces decision-making models like PACED, emphasizing cost-benefit analysis and critical thinking for sound financial choices.

  • Credit card terms and interest rates
  • Online purchasing security
  • Cost-benefit analysis in spending decisions

Standard 9: Fraud & Identity Theft

Focuses on setting financial goals, budgeting, emergency savings, and planning for major life events to ensure financial preparedness.

  • Types of financial fraud
  • Preventing identity theft
  • Emergency savings and financial protection

Standard 10: Renting vs. Buying

Covers economic concepts like supply and demand, inflation, and government roles to understand how economic trends affect personal finances.

  • Renting vs. homeownership pros and cons
  • Mortgage basics and real estate considerations
  • Inflation and financial planning

Standard 11: Risk Management & Insurance

Covers insurance and strategies to mitigate financial risks, emphasizing selecting coverage to maintain financial stability.

  • Health, auto, and life insurance
  • Managing financial risk in different life stages
  • Evaluating insurance needs and coverage

Standard 12: Gambling

Explores gambling risks, probability, and its social and emotional impacts to promote informed decision-making and awareness.

  • The psychology of gambling
  • Financial risks associated with gambling
  • Probability and statistical concepts

Standard 13: Bankruptcy

Covers debt management strategies, including consolidation, negotiation, and rebuilding credit, to regain financial control.

  • Types of bankruptcy (Chapter 7, 11, 13)
  • Impact of bankruptcy on credit
  • Rebuilding credit after bankruptcy

Standard 14: Charitable Contributions

Explores forms of giving, evaluating charities, benefits of philanthropy, and tax advantages to encourage responsible contributions.

  • The importance of charitable giving
  • Evaluating the legitimacy of charities
  • Tax benefits of donations
