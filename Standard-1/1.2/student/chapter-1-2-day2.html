Chapter 1.2: Paying for Post-Secondary Education - Learning Lab

Welcome to the Learning Lab! Today, we'll build on yesterday's exploration of education financing options, creating a personalized education financing strategy.

Learning Objectives

By the end of this Learning Lab, you will be able to:

  • Create a comprehensive education financing strategy based on your educational goals
  • Analyze different loan scenarios to understand potential debt and repayment implications
  • Develop a financial aid application timeline
  • Formulate a concrete action plan that includes specific funding sources and strategies to minimize debt
  • Build a scholarship search strategy tailored to your strengths and background

Introduction

Financing post-secondary education is a complex and personal decision that requires careful planning. Today's Learning Lab will guide you through creating a comprehensive financing strategy tailored to your specific educational goals and financial situation.

We'll start by using interactive tools to explore different funding options and their long-term implications. Then you'll develop a concrete action plan, including a timeline for financial aid applications, scholarship search strategies, and debt minimization techniques.

Important Note

The education financing strategy you create today is for learning purposes. As you approach actual post-secondary education decisions, please consult with financial aid advisors, guidance counselors, and your family to make informed choices based on your specific circumstances.

Connection to Day 1

In Day 1, we explored different financial aid options (scholarships, grants, loans, work-study) and the FAFSA process. We also looked at how to prioritize funding sources to minimize debt through the funding pyramid. Today, we'll use that knowledge to create a personalized education financing strategy with specific action steps.

Standard Repayment Plan

Fixed monthly payments over a 10-year term.

  • Fixed payment amount for the entire repayment period
  • Pays off the loan in the shortest time
  • Results in paying the least interest overall
  • Higher monthly payments than other plans

Graduated Repayment Plan

Payments start low and increase every two years.

  • Lower payments at the beginning of your career
  • Payments increase as your income is expected to rise
  • Still pays off the loan in 10 years
  • Pay slightly more interest than the Standard plan

Extended Repayment Plan

Extends repayment term up to 25 years.

  • Lower monthly payments than Standard or Graduated plans
  • Available for borrowers with more than $30,000 in loans
  • Can have fixed or graduated payments
  • Significantly higher total interest paid over the life of the loan

Income-Driven Repayment Plans

Monthly payments based on income and family size.

  • Several options: IBR, PAYE, REPAYE, ICR
  • Monthly payments typically 10-20% of discretionary income
  • Loan forgiveness after 20-25 years of qualifying payments
  • Must recertify income and family size annually
  • May result in paying more interest over time
  • Forgiven amount may be taxable as income
